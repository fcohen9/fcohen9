Maryland Vehical Assosication App Redesign

Redesign of Maryland Vehical Association forms, with the target user being persons over the age of 65

process

Since my application was supposed to cater to those over the age of 65, I began by creating a user persona.

I had some trouble beginning to generate visuals for this project, so I started by making a mindmap.

Once I did that and understood the content I was dealing with better, I drew up some quick wireframes.

After completing some wireframes, I began to lay elements out.

Initially, I settled on this wordmark and color scheme. However, upon further reflection, I realized that my design was ignoring my user's needs, as it was very hard to read because of the small type (and the fact that I was using a display typeface for copy) and low contrast with the text vs the background. To solve this, I introduced new colors, changed my typefaces, and altered my grid.

delieverables

I chose to use a bright blue and a bright orange for both the contrast that these colors provide, but also to make this application a little more fun, since the MVA is known for being dull and depressing. I chose to use the typeface Brown, as it is round and friendly.

I altered my wordmark so that it would be more adaptable and able to be used both on a loading screen at a larger scale and within the header.

Based on my user persona, I decided it was important for there to be a lot of check points within the app so that the user would be aware of where they are within the application.

I continued to think about how to best lead elderly users through the application, and decided to have a review screen after every section before the submit button appears, so that users can double check their information and make any changes that need to be made. The review screens appear after every section so that the user doesn't get confused or overwhelmed at the end when they are trying to submit their final forms.

Finally, I wanted to give the user a little reward for completing their forms (in addition to reminding them where they are in the process). I chose to create some illustrations that appear when a user has completed forms.
