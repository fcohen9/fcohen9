Lost City Website Redesign

process

we began this project by asking our "stakeholder" some general questions, like what they felt the biggest problems with the current site were, and what they liked and wanted to keep.

  • What do you like most about current site?
  • Aesthetic, retro sci fi
  • What are the main problems?
  • when was it last updated?
  • hierarchy is bad
  • it’s not accessible (a bunch of images that don’t have alt descriptions)
  • it is closed
  • not responsive
  • What do you think our target audience wants?
  • Menu and hours very easily
  • What would a sucessful redesign look like?
  • More easy to navigate and more accessible

Then, I made a priority guide based on this information:

Hours
Menus
Location
Directions
Contact: 410 547 LOST
Specialties
Last updated

Based on the feedback from my stakeholder and my priority guide, I then sketched out some wireframes:

From the wireframes, I started to do some more in depth layouts.

Initially I was going to use the old logo since my stakeholder liked the vibe of it, but ended up changing it to update it as I was updating the website. Originally, I kept the color scheme of the original website, just updating the images, buttons, and changing the hierarchy of the information. As I continued with the project, I ended up scrapping the dark color scheme for a lighter, more pleasant one, while still maintaining the comic book vibe.

I made some cute illustrations to go along with the comic book/ cozy vibe of the restaurant. They are decorative elements but also help the revamped website feel more cohesive, as that was a problem that my stakeholder mentioned.

deliverables

The final website was not the most important thing that I got from doing this project. I practiced making wireframes and doing research, but also practiced designing pixel perfect mockups with that knowledge in hand. I am improved my coding skills Immensely, to a point where I feel comfortable in semantic HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I also how to use pre-processors and static site generators, git and github, and about user personas and testing. Having this knowledge helps me so much as a product designer because I can design more practically and realistically -- and I learned that I really just love coding!
